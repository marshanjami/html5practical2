About the Books

History of the book

And the Mountains Echoed was published in 2013 as Khaled Hosseini’s third novel, following The Kite Runner (2003) and A Thousand Splendid Suns (2007).

Unlike his earlier works that focused mainly on one or two characters, this book uses an interconnected storytelling style, following multiple generations and locations.

The novel reflects Hosseini’s Afghan heritage, blending themes of family, sacrifice, and migration with stories set in Afghanistan, Paris, and the United States. It received positive reviews for its emotional depth and complex narrative, further establishing Hosseini as an important contemporary author writing about Afghanistan.

Main Themes in the book

  • Family bonds
  • Love and sacrifice
  • Separation and reunion
  • Impact of decisions on future generations

Top Reasons I Recommend This Book

  1. The writing is very moving and powerful.
  2. The characters feel realistic and relatable.
  3. The story connects different places and generations.
  4. It teaches the value of compassion and family.

Glossary

Main character, a devoted brother who loves his sister Pari.
Pari
Abdullah’s younger sister, separated from him as a child.
Afghanistan
The main setting of the novel, rich in culture and history.
Sacrifice
A theme where characters give up something important for others.
Generations
The novel spans across different generations, showing long-term effects of choices.
